🏆 Top Picks

🚀 Feasibility Winner
Proxima Centauri b
At 4.24 light-years, it is the only exoplanet reachable with near-future technology (Breakthrough Starshot laser-propelled lightsail). While its habitability is the lowest of the four, its proximity makes it the only planet where a reconnaissance mission is conceivably launchable within the next century.
🌱 Habitability Winner
K2-18b
JWST detections of methane, carbon dioxide, and a possible dimethyl sulfide signal make K2-18b the strongest habitability candidate. Its Hycean classification (ocean-covered world with hydrogen-rich atmosphere) represents a fundamentally new category of potentially habitable world — one we didn't know to look for.

⚠ Risk Breakdown

Critical

Stellar Flaring

Proxima Centauri's 2017 superflare increased brightness 1,000× in 10 seconds. This likely stripped Proxima b's atmosphere. TRAPPIST-1's ultracool dwarf shows similar behavior. M-dwarf flaring is the single greatest threat to surface habitability across 3 of 4 candidates.

High

Propulsion Feasibility

Only Proxima b is reachable with proposed technology (lightsail at 0.2c). All other targets require fusion or antimatter drives that don't exist. TRAPPIST-1e at 40.7 ly would take ~700 years with theoretical fusion drives — a multi-generational commitment.

High

Atmospheric Integrity

Tidal locking in M-dwarf systems (Proxima b, TRAPPIST-1e, K2-18b) may cause atmospheric collapse on the dark side. Only Kepler-452b, with its Sun-like G2 star and likely 385-day orbit, avoids this — but at 1,400 ly, it's unreachable.

Medium

Biosignature False Positives

K2-18b's dimethyl sulfide signal is tantalizing but unconfirmed. Abiotic processes can produce CH₄ and CO₂. JWST needs more transit observations to reach 5σ confidence. The 2024 follow-up is ongoing.

Medium

Communication & Signal Delay

Even Proxima b has a 4.24-year one-way signal delay. Real-time mission control is impossible. Autonomous AI systems would be mandatory. For K2-18b (120 yrs) and Kepler-452b (1,400 yrs), the data returned would arrive for civilizations fundamentally different from the one that sent the probe.

💡 Positive Signals

🌏

JWST Spectral Breakthroughs

K2-18b's atmosphere shows carbon-bearing molecules — the first detection of organics in a habitable-zone exoplanet. This proves our instruments work.

💧

Hycean World Confirmation

The Hycean model (sub-Neptune with liquid water ocean) expands habitable candidates dramatically. K2-18b may be the first confirmed Hycean world.

☀️

Sun-Like Star Match

Kepler-452b orbits a G2 star — the same spectral class as our Sun — at nearly the same orbital period (385 days). It's the most "Earth-like" system found.

🔬

Breakthrough Starshot Viability

Laser-propelled lightsail technology could reach Proxima b in ~73 years. The $100M initiative is funded and prototyping. This is the closest we've come to interstellar mission capability.

✨ Surprise Findings

Kepler-452b's Age Paradox

Kepler-452 is approximately 6 billion years old — 1.5 billion years older than our Sun. If Kepler-452b developed life, it would have had 1.5 billion more years of evolution than Earth. Any biosphere would be radically more mature, potentially billions of years ahead. Yet the planet sits at 1,400 light-years, making it the least reachable — the most intriguing world is the most inaccessible.

K2-18b's Hycean Viability

K2-18b was dismissed as a mini-Neptune until 2023 JWST data revealed potential ocean surface conditions beneath its hydrogen envelope. It's the first exoplanet where we may have detected dimethyl sulfide — on Earth, this molecule is only produced by life. If confirmed, K2-18b would become the strongest evidence for extraterrestrial life, despite being classified as "unlikely habitable" just five years ago. The Hycean model has rewritten the habitability playbook.

📜 Final Mission Brief

The exoplanet mission priority landscape reveals a fundamental paradox that defines interstellar exploration: feasibility and habitability are inversely correlated. Proxima Centauri b — our nearest neighbor at 4.24 light-years — sits at the bottom of the habitability scale, battered by its host star's violent flaring. Meanwhile, K2-18b — the most promising candidate for surface liquid water and potential biosignatures — requires propulsion technology that may not exist for centuries.

The strategic recommendation is a dual-track approach: invest in Breakthrough Starshot technology to reach Proxima b within a human lifetime (proving interstellar capability), while simultaneously deploying next-generation telescopes (Habitable Worlds Observatory, LUVOIR) to resolve K2-18b's biosignature question from Earth orbit. We don't need to go there to discover life — we need better eyes where we are.

The closest planet is the least habitable. The most habitable is unreachable. The path forward is both.
