# Day 2 - What Is Prompt Engineering

The skill of talking to AI effectively.

---

## What I Worked On

Ran the same topic through Claude twice — once with a lazy prompt, once with an engineered prompt. Compared the outputs side by side to see how much the input actually matters.

**Lazy prompt:** "What is prompt engineering?" — typed it as-is, no structure, no context.

**Engineered prompt:** Ran the lazy version through MetaPrompt (the Chrome extension from today's tool). It added a role, broke the request into 6 numbered sections, and defined what the output should cover.

The engineered output had tables, a before/after comparison, a pitfall chart, and a practice section. The lazy output was a decent paragraph-level explanation. Same AI, same topic, different results.

---

## Key Learnings

- The AI doesn't get smarter with a better prompt — it just has less guesswork to do
- A lazy prompt leaves gaps, and the AI fills them with the most generic assumptions
- Structure in the prompt (role, sections, format) directly translates to structure in the output
- The biggest differences show up in depth and actionability — not in whether the answer is "right," but in whether you can actually use it
- MetaPrompt is a useful starting point, but the real skill is learning to structure prompts yourself
