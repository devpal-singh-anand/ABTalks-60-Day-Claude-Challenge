# Lazy Prompt

The prompt I typed without thinking about structure or context. Just the question as it came to mind — no role, no format, no nothing. This is what most people type when they first use an AI.

---

## The Prompt

```
What is prompt engineering?
```

---

## The Output

Prompt engineering is the practice of designing and refining inputs (prompts) given to AI language models to get the most accurate, relevant, and useful outputs.

Think of it like knowing the right questions to ask — a well-crafted prompt can dramatically improve the quality of an AI's response, while a vague one can lead to generic or unhelpful results.

Key aspects include:

**Clarity and specificity** — Being precise about what you want. "Write a 3-paragraph summary of climate change for a 10-year-old" will outperform "explain climate change."

**Context-setting** — Giving the model background it needs, like a role ("Act as a senior software engineer...") or constraints ("respond only in JSON").

**Examples (few-shot prompting)** — Showing the model examples of desired input/output pairs so it can pattern-match to your expectations.

**Chain-of-thought** — Asking the model to "think step by step," which improves reasoning on complex problems.

**Negative constraints** — Telling the model what not to do (e.g., "don't include jargon") can be as useful as positive instructions.

**Iteration** — Prompt engineering is rarely one-and-done; it involves testing, observing outputs, and refining.

It's a skill that sits at the intersection of communication, critical thinking, and an understanding of how language models work. As AI becomes more embedded in workflows, prompt engineering is increasingly valuable for developers, writers, analysts, and really anyone using these tools regularly.
