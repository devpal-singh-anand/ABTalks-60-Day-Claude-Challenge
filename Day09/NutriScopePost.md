Day-9/60

Built NutriScope — an AI nutrition analytics dashboard — in two focused stages. MVP first, then enhanced. The lesson? One big prompt = one big mess. Two focused prompts = two working products.

◆ MVP → 20 foods, 10 nutrients, Chart.js dashboard, deficiency detection, smart recommendations
◆ Enhanced → 60 foods, 20 nutrients, CSV upload, 2-day meal planner, risk analysis (7 health risks with 🟢🟡🔴), radar & area charts, meal-specific tips, IFCT/USDA/NIN sources

◆ What changed between versions:
◆ 20 → 60 foods | 10 → 20 nutrients | 3 → 5 charts
◆ Basic recos → Advanced + meal-specific + timing tips
◆ No risk analysis → 7 health risk indicators
◆ That gap = exactly what iterative development delivers

◆ Biggest insight: Build something that works first, then make it better. The MVP was tight — Claude delivered cleanly. The enhancement only specified what was new — it layered complexity without breaking what worked. Same way real developers build software.
