## 15 30-DAY ACTION PLAN
This sprint is designed to validate the core hypothesis — that Indian students will actively practice mock
interviews with AI feedback and pay for it — before investing in full voice-AI development.
WEEK 1 (Days 1–7): Customer Discovery & Problem Validation
Day 1–2 Conduct 15 structured interviews with tier-2/3 engineering students. Use Typeform or
WhatsApp to gather responses. Key questions: How do you currently prepare? What would
you pay for? What's your biggest fear in interviews?
Day 3–4 Analyze responses. Identify top 3 pain points and preferred company targets (TCS, Infosys,
Wipro likely top 3). Draft your core positioning statement.
Day 5–6 Map out 50 TCS-specific, 50 Infosys-specific, and 30 HR/behavioral questions from publicly
available data. This is your initial Q-bank.
Day 7 Set up a simple Notion or Google Sheets "MVP" — you play the AI, interview 5 students
manually, give feedback. This is a Wizard of Oz test.
WEEK 2 (Days 8–14): MVP Build — Text-Based Mock Interview
Day 8–9 Build a minimal text-based mock interview on WhatsApp Business API or a simple web
form. Use Claude/GPT-4 API as the interview engine with your Q-bank as context.
Day 10–11 Implement basic scoring rubric: relevance (1–10), communication clarity (1–10), STAR
structure adherence. Display scores in a readable format after each session.
Day 12–13 Run the MVP with 30 students. Track: completion rate, session duration, satisfaction score
(0–10), and NPS.
Day 14 First "retention check": how many came back for session 2 without prompting? Target:
40%+.
#### WEEK 3 (Days 15–21): Distribution & Monetization Experiment
Day 15–16 Post in 10 college WhatsApp groups, LinkedIn, and Twitter/X with a clear "Free AI Mock
Interview — TCS/Infosys Patterns" hook. Measure clicks and signups.
Day 17–18 After free trial (3 sessions), show a simple Razorpay payment page (n199/month).
Measure conversion rate.
Day 19–20 Interview 10 users who did NOT convert. Document objections. Adjust messaging or
pricing based on real feedback.
Day 21 First revenue check. Even 5 paid users = signal. Zero conversions = pivot on
pricing/messaging immediately.
WEEK 4 (Days 22–30): Accelerator Prep & Voice MVP Planning
Day 22–24 Compile all data: interviews conducted, session completion rates, NPS scores, conversion
rates, user quotes. This becomes your traction deck.
Day 25–26 Draft your Y Combinator or 100X.VC application. Use this report + your 30-day data as the
validation section.

### 🏗 BYOK / BYOS ARCHITECTURE ADVANTAGE
One important architectural decision discussed after the report was completed is that PrepPal AI is
being designed around a Bring-Your-Own-AI (BYOK) and Bring-Your-Own-Storage (BYOS) model.
Instead of routing all AI requests through a central platform account, users are expected to connect
their own AI providers (OpenAI, Anthropic, Gemini, etc.) using their own API keys. This means AI token
usage is billed directly to the user rather than the platform.
Likewise, long-term storage is intended to leverage user-controlled storage providers where possible.
This significantly reduces operational costs compared to traditional AI SaaS platforms and changes the
primary sustainability challenge from AI token expenses to platform maintenance, infrastructure,
community support, and continued product development.
The architecture aligns with PrepPal AI's long-term goal of remaining accessible, community-
supported, and cost-efficient while scaling to a larger user base.
### REVISED HONEST ASSESSMENT
With BYOK/BYOS, even at 5,000 users the platform burn stays under ₹10K/mo — easily sustainable
through donations and small sponsorships alone. The scaling risk is no longer financial — it's
operational: founder capacity, community management, monitoring, and product development. This is a
dramatically better position than traditional AI SaaS platforms where token costs scale linearly with
users.
