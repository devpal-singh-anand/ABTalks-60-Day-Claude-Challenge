Recommendations to Improve Interview Chances
🏆
Pursue CompTIA Security+ within 90 days
It appears in 75%+ of job postings as a preferred or required cert. Your existing Cisco Networking Essentials and IBM certs make the study significantly faster. This single action unlocks the most roles.
🛡️
Get hands-on SIEM experience via free labs
Microsoft Sentinel has a free 30-day Azure trial. Splunk offers a free developer instance. TryHackMe's SOC Level 1 path (~40 hrs) gives structured SIEM + MITRE ATT&CK practice. Add this to your resume as "lab experience".
💻
Build and publish 2–3 security Python projects on GitHub
You already have Scapy and Pandas skills. Create a network packet analyzer, a log parser, or a simple threat detection script. A public GitHub profile is a strong differentiator at the entry level.
🎯
Participate in CTFs or bug bounty platforms
HackerOne explicitly requires vuln disclosure experience. TryHackMe, HackTheBox, and PicoCTF are free. Even 2–3 documented challenge completions differentiate you from candidates with only classroom knowledge.
📄
Tailor your resume for each role type
You have a dual profile (security + data analytics). For SOC roles, lead with Cisco, IBM CyberSec, NIIT Cyber Ops. For data security roles, lead with Python + Pandas + predictive modeling. One generic resume will underperform both.
🔗
Optimize your LinkedIn for recruiter searches
Add "SOC Analyst", "Security Operations", "Threat Detection" to your headline and About section. Recruiters at MNCs like Mimecast and Mastercard actively search these keywords.
Overall Fit Assessment
Your profile is a solid entry-level match for L1 SOC, Junior InfoSec Analyst, and Security Operations roles. You meet the foundational knowledge bar — the gap is primarily in hands-on SIEM tool experience and the Security+ cert, both of which are closeable in under 3 months.
