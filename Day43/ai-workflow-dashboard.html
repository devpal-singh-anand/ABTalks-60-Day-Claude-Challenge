❌ Common Mistakes
  • ✕Using AI without human review — robotic, generic posts destroy trust.
  • ✕Posting without a hook — first 2 lines determine 80% of reach.
  • ✕Ignoring analytics — repeat what works, cut what doesn't.
  • ✕Copy-pasting generic content — your audience detects it instantly.
  • ✕Over-using hashtags — 3–5 relevant tags outperform 20 spammy ones.
  • ✕Not engaging with others' content — LinkedIn rewards reciprocity.
✅ Best Practices
  • ✓Always edit AI output — add personal stories, opinions, and specific examples.
  • ✓Lead with a scroll-stopping hook — test 3+ variations per post.
  • ✓Post consistently — 3–5 times/week beats 7 inconsistent posts.
  • ✓Engage 15 min before & after posting — boosts algorithmic reach.
  • ✓Use the 80/20 rule — 80% value, 20% promotion.
  • ✓Repurpose top posts — turn a winner into a carousel, video, and article.
🔒 Security Checklist
  • 🔑Store all API keys in environment variables — never commit to git.
  • 🔑Review AI content for hallucinations — verify names, stats, and quotes.
  • 🔑Fact-check all claims — credibility is your most valuable asset.
  • 🔑No sensitive company data in prompts — assume prompts may be logged.
  • 🔑Use OAuth for LinkedIn API — avoid storing passwords.
  • 🔑Audit third-party tool permissions quarterly.
⚖️ Authenticity Guardrails
  • ♦Voice fingerprint — feed Claude 5 of your best posts to lock your tone.
  • ♦Personal story ratio — every post needs at least one specific, lived detail.
  • ♦Opinion marker — take a clear stance; fence-sitting kills engagement.
  • ♦AI disclosure — be transparent when AI materially shaped a post.
  • ♦Review SLA — never publish within 10 minutes of drafting; sleep on it.
  • ♦Diversity check — vary formats, lengths, and topics weekly.
