# When AI Hallucinates — And Why Human Judgment Still Wins

Day 14 of #60DayClaudeChallenge revealed something important about AI that not enough people talk about.

**What happened:**
→ Used Claude to analyze a job listing — scored it 78/100 risk, ❌ AVOID
→ AI's top flag (10/10 severity): "Company is a digital marketing agency hiring a cyber analyst — not a security firm"
→ Sounded logical. Clean pattern. Strong signal.

**The problem:**
→ Any company can hire cybersecurity professionals — regardless of industry
→ A law firm, a bakery, a marketing agency — all have data to protect and systems to secure
→ The company type was never the real issue — the missing infrastructure, absent team details, and undefined scope were

**The correction:**
→ Human review caught the mistake in after going through it.
→ Reframed the flag, reduced severity from 10 → 6
→ Risk score dropped: 78 → 65
→ Verdict changed: ❌ AVOID → ⚠️ Apply with Caution

**The lesson:**
→ AI finds patterns — but doesn't always understand context
→ It can tell you something looks unusual, but can't always tell if unusual = bad accurately.
→ AI is a powerful first pass, but the human reviewing it makes it actually useful in real world.

**Why this matters:**
→ People who just vibe code blindly don't get far
→ Those who can quickly review, catch mistakes, correct them, and move forward — they ship quality work faster
→ The tool is only as sharp as the mind holding it
